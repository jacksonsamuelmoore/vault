### Starter

- Currency union:  Distinguished from a full monetary union, this is where parties share a common currency but no other aspects of monetary policy

### Features of Monetary Unions

- Shared monetary policy:
    - Common bank rate
    - Currency issued by central bank
- Common currency
- Shared Central Bank
- Fiscal Harmonisation
    - Members must meet criteria before joining and maintain similar economies to the rest of the group

### Convergence Criteria

Measure how able they are to join the union:

- Price stability
- Sound public finances, to ensure sustainable economies
- Exchange-rate stability, showing long-term management abilities
- Long-term interest rates, assessing how well they'll do without interest rates as a tool

### Costs and benefits

Benefits:

- Lower transaction costs
- Greater price transparency
- Eliminate exchange uncertainty
- Increased trade and investment within the union
- Promotes discipline in managing finances

Costs:

- Loss of independent monetary policy: Exchange rates and QE handled by central bank
- High costs of leaving
- Cannot restore competitiveness through devaluation due to one single currency
- No lender of last resort
