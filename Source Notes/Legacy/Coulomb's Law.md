### Coulomb's law

For newton's law:

$F={-GMm\over r^2}$

Objects exert force that is

- That is proportional to the product of the masses
- and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them

So for coulomb's law:

$F={kQq\over r^2}={Qq\over 4\pi \varepsilon_0 r^2}$

Where Q and q are charge, r is distance between charges and $\varepsilon_0\approx8.85*10^{-12}F/m$

Objects exert force that is

- That is proportional to the product of the charges
- and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them

The electric permittivity of a medium describes how much electric field (more correctly, flux) is generated per unit charge in it (to do with the alignment of molecules with the field
