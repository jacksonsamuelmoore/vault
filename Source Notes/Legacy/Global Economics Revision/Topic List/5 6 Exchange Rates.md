# 5.6 Exchange Rates

What you should know: • What is an exchange rate (ER)?

Floating ER:
• What is a floating exchange rate system? Can you give some examples of countries that use this system?
• What is the difference between an appreciation and depreciation?
• What determines the demand for a currency?
• What determines the supply of a currency?
• What determines the equilibrium ER? Can you illustrate the equilibrium ER using a demand-supply diagram?
• Using a demand-supply diagram and step-by-step analysis, can you explain the impact of the following changes on the equilibrium ER:
    ◦ Exports?
    ◦ Imports?
    ◦ FDI?
    ◦ Portfolio investments?
    ◦ Interest rates?
    ◦ Expectations/speculation?
• What are the advantages and disadvantages of a floating ER system?

Fixed ER:
• What is a fixed (AKA pegged) ER? Can you give some examples of countries that use this system?
• Why does a country need large domestic and foreign currency reserves to maintain a fixed ER system?
• Can you use a demand-supply diagram to illustrate how it works?
• What is the difference between a devaluation and revaluation? Why might they happen?
• What are the advantages and disadvantages of a fixed ER system?

Managed ER:
• What is a managed ER system? Can you give some examples of countries that use this system?
• Using a demand-supply diagram, can you explain how the following tools used to manage an ER:
    ◦ Interest rates?
    ◦ Quantitative Easing?
    ◦ Taxation?
    ◦ Buying and selling of domestic/foreign currencies?
• What are the advantages and disadvantages of a managed ER system?
